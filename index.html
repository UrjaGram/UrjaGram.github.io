What is this
Two-phase mission — field to code
📋

Phase 1 — Ground Truth the DPR

Visit GP Jhansa (25 km from NIT), collect real household data, run energy surveys, and populate the Detailed Project Report with actual numbers. Supported by PM Shri School students and SHG women members.

1–7 June Rs 2,000 stipend 2 field visits
⚡

Phase 2 — Design & Build VET-OS

Using your own field data, independently design and prototype the Village Energy Transition Operating System. Solar tracking, biogas monitoring, carbon MRV, RESCO billing, GP dashboard. AI evaluates the winner.

8 Jun – 26 Jul Rs 8,000 stipend 7 required modules

Why this matters: The Jhansa project will transition 1,745 households to clean energy — solar, biogas, clean cooking. The VET-OS is the digital backbone: monitoring, billing, carbon credits, and real-time dashboards. Your prototype could become the production system. All three teams compete on the same ground-truth data. The winner's design is taken forward.

Question 1 — Baseline & Energy Profile
How many households does the Jhansa DPR cover? Describe the village's cooking-energy profile — how many households fall into each fuel category (mixed LPG-biomass, biomass-only, LPG-only) and what is the LPG-to-biomass energy-use ratio for mixed households? *
Question 2 — Energy Expenditure
What is the total annual household energy expenditure in Jhansa (electricity + cooking fuels) at baseline? What is the projected cumulative 10-year expenditure if no transition occurs? *
Question 3 — Technical Solution & Solar
What is the total confirmed rooftop solar portfolio proposed for Jhansa? Break it down into residential and school components (MW and number of systems). What is the estimated annual solar generation? *
Question 4 — Cooking Technology Stack
The DPR proposes a "Household Cooking Technology Stack" rather than a single cooking solution. Name at least four cooking options included in this stack and explain why a diversified approach is needed. *
Question 5 — Transition Pathway
The DPR describes a two-track household energy transition pathway (electricity and cooking fuel). Outline the three phases for each track and the target outcomes by FY 2035-36. *
Question 6 — Project Cost & PM Surya Ghar
What is the gross project cost range? What is the PM Surya Ghar subsidy per household system, and what net funding requirement remains after this central subsidy? What national conversion-rate problem does VET-OS address? *
Question 7 — Carbon Credits
How many tonnes of CO2 can the Jhansa rooftop solar portfolio avoid per year? Show the calculation method (generation in GWh, grid emission factor, result in tCO2e). *
Question 8 — VET-OS & Implementation
What is VET-OS and what are its key functions in the Jhansa project? Name the three local implementation engines described in the DPR and explain how each contributes to the project. *
